In this experiment you will investigate moments of inertia by applying a torque to a disk that is free to rotate. The torque comes from a string wrapped around a pulley on the disk; the string passes over a second pulley and has a mass hanging from it.

Lab Instructions

The moment of inertia I of a body is a measure of how hard it is to get it rotating about some axis. The moment I is to rotation as mass m is to translation. The larger the I, the more work required to get the object spinning, just as the larger the mass m, the more work required to get it moving in a straight line. Alloy rim wheels on bicycles have a lower I than steel rim wheels and so are easier to get spinning, making fast bicycle acceleration easier.
